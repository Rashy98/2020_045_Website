Literature Survey


A wide range of research is being performed around the world in response to the various challenges that have arisen as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic. These research mostly use IoT for their innovations due its popularity in the current world. Some of these studies are discussed in this section of this paper.

DacNhuong Le et al. [2] introduce a novel IoT-enabled Depth-wise separable convolution neural network (DWS-CNN) with Deep support vector machine (DSVM) for COVID-19 diagnosis and classification in their article. The proposed DWS-CNN model incorporates a collection of processes, including data acquisition, Gaussian filtering (GF) based preprocessing, feature extraction, and classification, to detect both binary and multiple classes of COVID-19. Initially, patient data is acquired via IoT devices and transferred to a cloud server during the data collecting stage. Furthermore, the GF approach is used to remove any noise that may be present in the image. Similar to [2], T. Ozturk et al. [6] present a new approach for automatic COVID-19 identification using raw chest X-ray pictures. The suggested approach is designed to offer diagnostics for both binary and multi-class classification in identifying COVID, pneumonia and no findings. This system uses DarkNet technology for accurate predictions of convolutional layers. The purpose of this study is to see if radiology can be used to verify the scan method. The heatmaps generated by automated models were evaluated by radiologists. Fivefold cross-validation was utilized to test the functionality of the newly built method. The highest level of accuracy has been obtained and reported for both binary and multiclass categorization.

Similar to DacNhoung Le et al., a real-time COVID-19 detection and monitoring system is proposed by Otoom et al. [3] in their work. This system employs an Internet of Things (IoTs) framework that uses wearable devices to collect real-time symptom data from users in order to identify suspected coronavirus cases as early as possible, as well as machine learning techniques to monitor the treatment response of those who have already recovered from the virus and to get a better understand of the virus's nature by collecting and analyzing relevant data. The paper CIoTVID, co-authored by A. Ramallo-González, A. González-Vidal, and A. Skarmeta [5], also offers an integrated approach based on IoT technologies that can act as opportunistic health data gathering agents for combatting the COVID-19 pandemic. This system uses an intelligent bot for the preliminary diagnosis of the patients where the bot collects the data using IoT devices. S. Karmore et al. [7] designed a Medical Diagnose Humanoid (MDH) that uses a mobile robotic system to do a complete diagnostic test to see if a person is infected with COVID-19. The data provided by the humanoid's sensors was combined with real-time data sensing and processed using machine learning. The system processes blood samples, X-rays, and sensory input from the test subject using machine learning techniques. It has sensors that determine body temperature and respiratory rate, such as a temperature sensor and an Airflow breathing sensor. Both of these sensors must be in contact with the patient. Moreover, Amani Kallel and her colleagues [8] have created a bespoke framework for COVID - 19 monitoring and prediction. The framework, according to the authors, incorporates hardware devices that might be medical devices like real-time polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR), X-ray machines like CT scans, or non-medical devices like wearable devices, drones, and so on. Another study by Cacovean et al. [9] uses a wearable device to collect and monitor a person's temperature and heart rate for a week. To evaluate whether the person is suspected of being infected with the Coronavirus, machine learning techniques are employed to examine the collected data. Furthermore, in their study, Hamid Mukhtar et al. [10] used a similar strategy of using wearable gadgets to determine potential suspects. The hardware component, according to the authors, is made up of two parts: one that wraps around the arm and the other that goes around the person's throat.

Other than identifying potential patients, some studies are carried out to minimize the spread of the virus among people. Petrovic and Kocic [11] created a cheap IoT-based system to improve COVID-19 indoor safety by measuring temperature, identifying face masks, and assessing social distancing. To achieve the aforementioned objectives, they are employing non-contact methods. To achieve contactless temperature measurement, they employ an Arduino Uno with an infrared sensor or a thermal camera, while mask detection and social distancing checks are performed using computer vision techniques on a camera-equipped Raspberry Pi. The next section of this research compares and contrasts already available approaches for identifying probable COVID-19 patients with the suggested system in this study.

Since SARS-CoV-2 is a novel coronavirus strain, there was no effective IoT-based solution available at the start of the outbreak. The approaches for identifying COVID-19 patients, such as PCR and Rapid Antigen testing, were not appropriate in all cases. As a result, many public venues and organizations have begun to monitor body temperature in order to identify potential COVID-19 infected persons and thereby control the disease's spread. With the appearance of COVID-19 positive individuals who showed no signs of elevated body temperature, the method's validity was called into question. Researchers devised numerous techniques and devices to provide a superior replacement for the current methodology. Yet, most of them are not better enough to control the disease.

According to DacNhuong Le and his colleague’s research (A [2]), they employed an X-ray-based approach to differentiate COVID-19 positives. According to the authors, they collected patient data using IoT devices and then uploaded it to a cloud server. Similar concept of using X-ray images have adopted by the T. Ozturk et al (D [6]) in their research as well. Both These systems will not be suitable for daily use in an organization because of the usage of X-ray medium. Aside from that, the operational costs and influence on personnel may produce a slew of other issues at an organizational level.

Otoom et al. present a new technique to pre-identify probable COVID-19 infected patients in their study (B [3]). According to the authors, they have used a wearable device to read the individuals. These data are then transferred to a cloud server for irregularities to be detected. The research (F [9]) by Cacovean et al. also followed a similar methodology of employing wearable devices. Even though both the methodologies maintain a contactless approach in finding potential COVID-19 positive persons, it can assist in a variety of unforeseen organizational situations. It's possible that not every employee has a wearable gadget. As a result, the system is unable to monitor such individuals, potentially spreading the virus to others.

Even though both the methodologies maintain a contactless approach in finding potential COVID-19 positive persons, it can assist in a variety of unforeseen organizational situations. It's possible that not every employee has a wearable gadget. As a result, the system is unable to monitor such individuals, potentially spreading the virus to others.

A. Ramallo-González, A. González-Vidal, and A. Skarmeta's designed smart robot (C [5]) is a unique IoT-based solution for controlling disease spread. The robot has improved the contactless approach of identifying positive people by allowing them to be monitored remotely. As a result, this system would be perfect for monitoring patients in COVID-19 wards of hospitals. However, due to the nature of mobility, this will not be suitable for an organization.

S. Karmore et al. (E [7]) used robots to distinguish COVID-19 positive persons in their study. The authors claim to have employed "extreme testing" such as blood tests and X-ray pictures. Apart from that, the authors have said that the system's sensors must be in direct contact with the test subject. As a result, this technique fails to address the contactless approach and, due to the utilization of rigorous testing, will not be suitable for an organization.

According to Amani Kallel and her colleagues' research (G [8]), using hardware components that may be medical equipment, such as real-time polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR), makes the system much more complex and difficult to manage. As a result, companies may face a slew of other issues when it comes to controlling and maintaining the device.

When it comes to Hamid Mukhtar et al (H [10]) created approach, they used a similar notion of wearable gadgets to distinguish COVID-19 positive persons. The device should be worn around the arm and throat, according to the authors, which is in violation of the contactless approach. As a result, this approach is unsuitable for virus control at the organizational level.

Furthermore, Petrovic and Kocic have investigated and created a low-cost IoT-based solution (I [11]) to monitor if persons adhere to government-imposed laws such as wearing a face mask and maintaining a social distance. This technique can be used to see if employees are following the rules, but it is not reliable enough to detect those who may be infected with COVID-19.

When the foregoing facts are considered, it is evident that no adequate system has yet been developed to replace the current practice of monitoring body temperature to identify probable COVID-19 infected persons at an organizational level. As a result, the InCov chamber was created to be used at the entrance of any organization while addressing the aforementioned challenges. The above explanations are summarized in the table below.

Research Problem

The novel coronavirus has quickly spread around the world. This virus spreads between people when an affected person is in close contact with another person. This is a respiratory virus that is transferred by droplets produced when an infected person coughs or sneezes, as well as saliva or nasal discharge. According to current data, the virus transmits mostly amongst persons who are in close proximity to one another, generally within one meter (short-range). The virus can also transmit in cramped and/or poorly ventilated indoor environments where people spend extended periods. COVID-19 has prompted a worldwide lockdown, quarantine, and various limitations due to the virus's daily growing instances and deaths. To avoid a pandemic, governments have begun to impose bans on a variety of societal restrictions. The limitation of lockdown is at the forefront of these measures. Several difficulties come with being on lockdown. Many people have disobeyed recommendations about social isolation or social distance. It is uncertain if full lockdown can be maintained for months, and some countries alternate between strict and less harsh measures for the remainder of the year.

In some way, the pandemic has affected all industries and businesses throughout the world. People's economics, livelihoods, transportation, education, sports, entertainment, religion, social gatherings and interactions, research, companies, politics, and physical and mental health have all been affected by the COVID-19 epidemic. Its expansion has left national economies and companies calculating the expenses, as governments struggle to implement additional lockdown measures to stop the virus from spreading further. Tourism and hospitality are among the sectors of the economy that have come to a halt. Many people have lost their jobs or had their salaries reduced. The unemployment rate has risen in all major economies. Airlines have curtailed flights and consumers have canceled business trips and vacations, wreaking havoc on the travel sector. Millions of jobs have been lost in the hospitality industry, and many businesses have gone bankrupt.

Many research on the socio-economic implications of the global economy has addressed the global pandemic, namely COVID-19. Individuals are not allowed to go out on the streets, workplaces are closed, airplanes are prohibited, and people are sacked across about 90% of the world. COVID-19 is not just a medical problem, but it also has significant economic and societal ramifications for women. Women are withstanding the worst of COVID-19's economic and social consequences, even though everyone is experiencing unprecedented problems. The current COVID-19 epidemic has had serious psychological and sociological consequences on the wider population. Separation from loved ones, loss of independence, uncertainty about the disease's progression, and a sense of powerlessness are some of the aspects of the pandemic that affect the people more than others. Suicidal conduct is frequently linked to feelings of rage connected with a stressful situation that is prevalent among those who stayed in the most impacted places. Before COVID-19, people tend to interact with the society frequently. This was affected by the spread of the virus, resulting the society to live an isolated life. When limitations on how people run for parties and group settings are loosened, humans observe this. After COVID-19, the number of opportunities and places for sociability will increase.

Laboratory testing were used to identify the COVID-19 suspects. When testing facilities are few, however, they tend to be concentrated in or around the capital city, making it hard for residents in other parts of the country to receive prompt testing. PCR is the most precise and reliable diagnostic technique. While certain PCR tests are faster than others, the majority of them take several hours to complete. As a result, it is not a better solution for checking in at work on a daily basis. The results of such tests take several weeks to arrive in Sri Lanka. Furthermore, not everyone has the ability to do this testing, and the government lacks such resources. For the sake of safety, most businesses and organizations have resumed normal activities. As a result, the economy of the country tanked. As a result, organizations must reopen in order to regain the economy. Despite the fact that there are no reliable methods for identifying COVID-19 suspects, most organizations now just examine the entrants' body temperatures. However, individuals who test positive for COVID-19 do not appear to have a high body temperature. When it comes to Sri Lanka, we all know that the major cause for the 2nd wave of COVID-19 was due to the previously described inability to detect possible suspects before accessing the famed clothing manufacturing premises. Many individuals are dissatisfied with the present approach of detecting COVID-19 suspects by simply examining body temperature, according to our survey. As a result, the topic of "How to more efficiently detect possible COVID-19 suspects at the entrance of the considered organizations?" will be explored in this study.

Research Objectives

• Main Objective

To construct an intelligent chamber which is more efficient and precise than the existing method of checking only the body temperature used in identifying possible COVID-19 infected individuals at the entrance of a considered organization, by evaluating the symptoms, fever, cough, anosmia, and shortness of breath.

• Specific Objectives

The main focus of this section is on the secondary objectives that can be either specific or different to each component related to the product. Although they are described under the secondary objectives section, it is really important to keep in mind that these conditions should also function accordingly in order for the complete system to run. Also to ensure that the assumptions made during the project development period.

  1. Identifying the person

    2. Two subcomponents make up the person identification component. When a person enters the chamber, the system detects them and begins the recognizing process. To identify the person, the system employs two methods. The face recognition and voice recognition systems identify people and authorize the testing process to begin. Initially, the system uses face recognition to identify the person. If no match is found, the system starts the voice recognition process to identify the person. The purpose of identifying individuals who enter the chamber is to keep a record of their test results for further evaluations.

  2. Fever Detection

    3. The fever detection subcomponent is critical because 99% of symptomatic individuals have fever. The primary goal of fever detection is to check body temperature using a non-contact approach. A thermal camera is used in this system to measure the body temperature. When a person enters the chamber, the thermal camera automatically captures a thermal image. To classify the numerical value of the thermal image, the KNN classifier algorithm is used. The final result is generated in a matter of seconds.

  3. Anosmia Condition

    4. The main goal of this sub-component is to identify anosmia by using Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to compare the value of the person's given answer to the questions asked with the reading value of the fragrance inside the chamber. The system will ask a few questions regarding the odor within the chamber when the person enters. Natural Language Processing (NLP) methods are used to classify the recorded audio clips after the responses are captured using the microphone. The obtained results will be compared to the saturation level to see whether the person is a COVID-19 suspect or not.

  4. Cough detection and cough resemblance

    5. The primary purpose of this subsystem is to identify people by utilizing audio processing and transfer learning with a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to assess whether the acquired forced cough is compatible with the forced cough of COVID-19 patients. Regardless of whether the person is asymptomatic or symptomatic, the audio clip acquired from the individual's force-cough within the chamber with the usage of a microphone will be used to determine if the person is a potential COVID-19 suspect or not with a confidence level. This component of the system contains two sub-components. For both these components, the system should be able to record an audio of 5 seconds. Next, the system should be able to create a spectrogram for the audio acquired. Then the system should be able to detect if the acquired audio contains a cough or not. After that, if the audio contains a cough, then the system should be able to detect if the cough is a COVID-19 positive cough or not.

  5. Shortness of Breath Detection

    6. The component Shortness of Breath detection's purpose is to identify possible COVID-19 positive individuals using a contactless approach. The system aims to start the testing process automatically once a person enters to the chamber. The equipment is set up to take a 30-second reading of the respiration rate and generate a result at the conclusion of the 30th second. To identify this condition, system monitors the respiration rate of the individual using thermal image processing. Thus, a thermal camera is utilized in this system to measure the respiration rate while maintaining the contactless approach.

  6. Report Generation

    7. A report is a very useful tool for administration to track and analyze each employee/student on a daily basis. The report includes the person's name, identification number, and the daily results of the testing procedure. The report can be generated, viewed, and downloaded by the administrator. When the desired date is entered, the report can be downloaded as a PDF.

Methodology

Figure 1: System Diagram


This study was conducted to build an enclosed chamber at an organization's entrance, complete with equipment to identify possible COVID-19 suspects based on four common symptoms. Fever, cough, shortness of breath, and Anosmia (loss of smell) are among the symptoms considered in this study. The chamber includes a camera, a thermal camera, a microphone, and an air quality sensor for detecting or recognizing the symptoms stated.

Mentioned below are the five main components described in detail

1. Person Authentication

When a specific individual enters the chamber, he or she will be recognized and authorized to begin the testing process. The advantage of identifying a specific person is that you can keep track of their daily readings. When a need arises, this information can help both the individual and the health authorities. Individuals are identified using two distinct methods. Voice recognition is also used to identify people as a backup method if the first method fails.

Figure 2: System Diagram - Face Detection

2. Fever Detection​​

Fever is the most common of the corona virus's vital symptoms. Identifying individuals with elevated body temperatures provides a significant advantage in identifying potential suspects. When a person authenticates themselves for the first time, a thermal camera captures an image of their face. The image is captured using a FLIR C2 camera.

The captured image is processed to extract image features using the OpenCV python library and the Imutils python library. The image is then fed into a model that has been trained using the KNN classifier algorithm with the Tensorflow, SciKit Learn, and Keras backends to extract the numerical values in the image.

Figure 3 shows the system diagram for this component.

Figure 3: System Diagram - Fever Detection

3. Anosmia Detection​​

The inability to smell is known as Anosmia. Anosmia is recognized as one of the vital symptoms of COVID-19. The complete mechanism for detecting anosmia conditions is depicted in Figure 4. When the person enters the workplace, the system will diffuse the fragrance inside the chamber and ask questions about whether the person can smell it or not, with the person's response being recorded via the microphone. This audio will be transmitted to a Google API to convert voice to text, and the resulting text will be classified using a "Yes/No classifier" before being compared to the sensor reading to predict the overall outcome.

Figure 4: System Diagram - Anosmia Detection

4. Cough detection and resemblance

To assess if a person is a prospective COVID-19 patient, the proposed method employs a microphone and transfer learning with a CNN to analyze cough noises. Artificial Intelligence and the Internet of Things are the technologies used by this system. When someone walks inside the chamber, the system will ask them to cough into the microphone. The cough will then be recorded by the system. The captured audio will then be transformed into a spectrogram, which will be used by the system to determine whether the audio contains a cough using a CNN. The system will ask the person to cough again if no cough is detected. If a cough is detected, the spectrogram will be forwarded to another CNN to assess whether the person is a potential COVID-19 patient. The result is then passed to the main algorithm, along with its confidence level, whether positive or negative. Finally, the result is submitted to the database, together with the results of the other primary system components. This procedure is depicted graphically in Figure 5.

Figure 5: System Diagram - Cough Detection and Resemblance

5. Shortness of breath

Breathing difficulty is defined as shortness of breath. Shortness of breath occurs when a person's breathing rate exceeds that of a healthy adult, which is between 12 and 16 breaths per minute [1]. As a result, the person's respiration rate is used to identify the Shortness of Breath condition in this study. A thermally based medium is used in the system to accomplish this. The system consists of two separate hardware components for detection of SOB condition,

  • FLIR C2 thermal camera
  • Average web camera (W702 web camera)

It causes a significant temperature change at the person's nostril area during the inhalation process. The above-mentioned thermal camera is used to detect this temperature difference. However, to achieve a defect-free result, the nasal area must first be extracted. The web camera is used since the thermal image does not reflect the rich facial characteristics of the person within the chamber. The system determines the relevant x and y coordinates of the nose area using the web camera. The nostril area is then extracted by applying the determined coordinates to the thermal picture. Both cameras must be focused on the same object in order to apply the coordinates in this manner. The temperature difference near the nasal area is indicated by green color on the FLIR C2 thermal camera. Hence, the thermal image's extracted region is subjected to additional processing with the OpenCV python package in order to emphasize the important color change. As a result, the color change is represented as a binary picture.
